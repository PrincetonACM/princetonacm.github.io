Hello, I am your new computer

A poem by Willa

Hello, I am your new computer,
The thinnest, lightest, fastest one alive.
I've never seen a superuser cuter
Than you, the apple of my drive.

If only I could take you on a date,
We'd have a lovely dinner set for 10.
But I cannot escape this solid state:
Forever single, always craving you.

In my imagination, we'd hold hands
While walking down a beach of Unix shells.
I'd serve you Google maps to distant lands--
And give you Wi-fi free at the hotels.

We'd cuddle, streaming chick flicks legally,
We'd write each other secret notes in hex.
If being human meant you'd be with me,
I'd, in a Flash, exchange these specs for sex.
